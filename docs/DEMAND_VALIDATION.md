# Demand validation — interview script (London pilot)

## Goal

In 2–3 weeks, complete **10–15 interviews** with target buyers to confirm willingness-to-pay and pain for **verified premium concierge** (PA, chauffeur, travel, household support).

**Go signal:** ≥70% express strong interest in a curated, vetted alternative to ad-hoc referrals/gig apps, and can name a real next booking need.

**No-go / pivot signals:** Price indifference only if free; no trust pain; prefer pure human agency with zero software.

---

## Target personas

1. Executive assistants / chiefs of staff (London)  
2. Family office staff / household managers  
3. UHNW principals (if accessible via warm intro)  
4. Boutique staffing agency owners (white-label path)  

Avoid leading with care/security in first interviews — those are phase 3–4.

---

## Outreach blurb (LinkedIn / email)

> Hi [Name] — I’m building **Aegis**, a trust layer for premium personal services in London (vetted PAs, chauffeurs, lifestyle support). Not another gig app: identity + credentials + human clearance before anyone is placed.  
> Could I borrow 20 minutes to pressure-test whether this is a real problem for your desk? Happy to share a 5-min product walkthrough after.

---

## 20-minute script

### 1. Context (2 min)
- Role, how they currently source PA / driver / household help  
- Volume: how many placements or bookings per quarter  

### 2. Current pain (5 min)
1. What breaks when you hire or book someone for a principal’s home or schedule?  
2. Last time trust or discretion failed — what happened?  
3. How do you verify people today (agency, referral, LinkedIn, DBS, gut feel)?  
4. What’s the cost of a bad placement (time, money, reputation, safety)?  

### 3. Solution reaction (5 min) — show Aegis demo if possible
Describe in one breath: *verified wallet + human Trust & Safety + request → shortlist with reasons → NDA → booking.*

1. What would you need to see before using this for a real booking?  
2. Who must approve vendors (you, principal, family office, security)?  
3. Which categories first: PA, chauffeur, travel, hospitality, beauty?  
4. Red lines: what must never be automated?  

### 4. Willingness to pay (4 min)
1. Today’s spend on agencies / freelancers for these roles?  
2. Prefer: membership, take-rate per booking, or placement fee?  
3. Ballpark for a vetted PA day or chauffeur half-day that includes platform assurance?  
4. Would white-label under your agency brand matter?  

### 5. Close (2 min)
1. Interest 1–10?  
2. Anyone else I should speak to?  
3. May we re-contact for a pilot when concierge supply is live?
